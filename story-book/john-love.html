Bright Cars Story Book

John grew in his communication skills each day. He became more caring not only of his wife, but also of those around him. He learned how to be enthusiastic each day and to deeply care about all of the people he spoke with. Over time, John was greatly liked in his company. People would always come and talk to him, and in talking to everyone, John learned a lot about the different aspects of the company. He climbed the ranks of his company and was liked by all of his co-workers.

To his surprise, one day, Bright Car’s CEO, Jack Durand, called him aside and told him about this new opportunity that was going to change the world.

Jack: “John, I always knew that self-driving cars are the way to the future. That is why I started this company even though I did not have a single dollar in my pocket. Honestly, when I hired you, I did not see anything special, but I really needed workers who were willing to work for little. But the years have passed and you have proved yourself in this company. Through your warmth and generosity, everyone in the company has grown to respect you, and you have learned from everyone as well. And so I am excited to share with you that the government wants to hire our company to mass-produce the next generation of self-driving cars, and I am placing you in charge of this operation! You will work in direct contact with President Frank McDonald, the elected president of our wonderful country, the Happy States of Neopolis.”

John: “I don’t know what to say, Jack. Thank you so much, this is an honor. My wife will be so proud of me. I would love to lead this operation. Thank you for trusting me and I will not disappoint you.”

Jack: “Good. Now prepare a presentation tomorrow. I will announce your promotion to the entire company. Starting tomorrow you will have authority over every single employee in this company, except for me, of course. You will lead this project with the government, which is our most important project, and I will focus on managing our relations with our other private investors, and that other bureaucratic stuff that you don’t care to hear about.”

John: “Sounds good, boss. I will present myself in front of the company tomorrow morning.”

And so, after celebrating the news with his wife, John prepared his presentation and presented himself in front of the entire company. You missed the presentation but here is what his co-workers got from it:

John Love is utilitarian

He is willing to listen to everyone in the company and his priority is to maximize the number of satisfied people in the company. It is clear that he has to manage different expectations, for example, with the government, with private investors, with the engineers, with the factory workers, etc. But in general, John will do his best to keep everyone happy, even if it comes at the expense of the general productivity of the company and the project.

John Love is a deontologist

He has ideals which guide him in his life and in his conduct with people. John will certainly try to keep everyone happy, but his priority is with his co-workers, the people who will buy and ride in the cars his company produces, and the friends he made along the way. Also, there are certain things that John would never do, while there are other things that John would never fail to do. His ideals lead him in every decision, and he is inflexible about a decision once he’s made it. That makes him, the company, and this project highly efficient and productive.
