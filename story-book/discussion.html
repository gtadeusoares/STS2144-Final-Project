Project Discussion

This story book relates the story of John Love, a leader at the company Bright Cars, which is the leading self-driving car company in the country of Happy States of Neopolis, and Frank McDonnald, the elected president of Neopolis.

This semester, we discussed many different ethical frameworks. Some that stood out to me the most were utilitarianism and deontology. Utilitarianism seeks to maximize some good for the greatest number of people, and to minimize losses if possible. Deontology states that one’s actions are ruled by the principles that they hold, and that some actions are always right or wrong, regardless of the context.

In my story book, readers can choose whether John Love is more of a deontologist or an utilitarian. Since a person’s idea of the highest good may differ according to their personality, the first choice in the story book has the user choose whether John Love is more of a duties guy or a relationship guy. The second choice in the story book has the user pick whether John love is more of an utilitarian or deontologist. Depending on the answer to these questions, a different outcome will result.

We are then introduced to President Frank McDonnald. I thought I had to include a powerful political figure in this story because in class we spent a considerable time discussing whether the government should exert more pressure, intervene, or regulate the big tech companies in the United States and across the world. Therefore, the user may choose whether president Frank takes more of a controlling approach or a free-market approach.

President Frank and John Love eventually meet. The previous user choices have defined these characters’ personalities, and so they arrive at different conclusions in their meeting. The final choice for the user is to decide the role that social media, big tech and mass media will play in their implementation. I chose this because we spent a considerable amount of time in class discussing the influence social media, big tech, and mass media have on people.

In total, there are four possible outcomes. One where the country is controlled by the government, another where the country is run by businesses, another where the country becomes polarized due to social media, and a good ending, where people actually take the trouble to communicate transparently.
